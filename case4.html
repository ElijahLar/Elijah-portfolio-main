AfterKöp

Designing a calmer post-purchase workflow for Swedish Shopify stores

AfterKöp is a SaaS tool I built for Swedish Shopify stores that need a better way to handle returns, refunds, and support cases after the purchase. Instead of jumping between inboxes, Shopify, store policies, and internal notes, the merchant gets the full case in one place.

The goal was simple: make after-sales support feel less messy. Cases come in with the right context, AI helps with the first draft, and the full history can be saved as a dossier if the case needs follow-up later.

The Product Problem

For many smaller Shopify stores, the real mess starts after checkout. A return request might come through a support form, a refund question through email, and the actual order details sit somewhere else. Even fairly simple cases take too long because someone has to piece everything together before they can answer.

Repetitive support work

The same questions come back again and again, but the reply still often gets written from scratch.

Context lives in too many places

To answer one case properly, you often have to check the inbox, the order, the store policy, and whatever happened last time.

Compliance risk grows under pressure

When things are rushed, it is easier to miss deadlines, misread the customer's rights, or send a reply that creates more problems later.

Workflow and Product Structure

I designed AfterKöp around the case itself rather than a generic inbox. Once a store is connected to Shopify, the system pulls in the customer's message, relevant order data, category, status, and what the likely next step should be.

That makes the workflow much clearer: understand the case, review the basis for the recommendation, edit or approve the reply, and keep a record of what happened. The point was to make decisions faster without hiding the reasoning.

AI-Assisted Replies

One of the key features is AI-assisted reply drafting. The point was never to auto-send generic answers, but to give the merchant a solid first draft based on the actual case instead of a blank text field.

The draft is built from the customer's message, the case type, the order context, and the policy basis already attached to the case. From there, the merchant can edit it, save it, approve it, or keep working on it before anything is sent.

Compliance and Explainability

A big part of the product is that it shows why a recommendation exists, not just what the answer should be. The case view surfaces policy basis, store-specific support rules, and the official sources behind the recommendation.

That matters in Swedish e-commerce, where returns and refunds are not just support tasks, but legal ones too. If the system is going to help write replies, it also has to make the reasoning visible enough for the merchant to trust it.

Dossier and Follow-Up

AfterKöp can also export a PDF dossier for each case. It pulls together the summary, customer and order context, the basis for the recommendation, and a timeline of what happened.

That makes the case easier to revisit, share internally, or use if something turns into a dispute later. For me, this was an important part of the product because it made AfterKöp useful beyond just drafting replies.

Product Stack and Delivery

The product is hosted on Vercel, and Supabase handles login, account state, and tier logic like free versus pro. Shopify is connected directly so stores can pull in case and order context instead of entering everything by hand.

I also set up Google Tag Manager and Google Analytics 4 to track how people move through the product, which helps show where onboarding or case handling still needs work.

  • Hosting: Vercel
  • Backend and auth: Supabase
  • Commerce integration: Direct Shopify connection
  • Analytics: Google Tag Manager and Google Analytics 4
  • Core product areas: Case ingestion, AI reply drafting, policy guidance, dossier export, and account tiering

Reflection

What I like about this project is that it sits right between product design and real operations. It is not just about making support look cleaner. It is about helping stores move faster while still keeping context, documentation, and compliance intact.

If I keep developing this case, the next step is to add stronger product outcomes, usage numbers, or launch learnings so the page shows not just how the tool works, but what happened when people used it.
